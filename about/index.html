Kevon Cheung


A lot of people started their entrepreneurial journeys since young, perhaps by selling candies or instant noodles to schoolmates. For me, I was never an entrepreneurial kid. I was quite the opposite, I was the kid who lived by boundaries. And then at some point in my teenage life, I became obsessed with the idea of creating, bringing true values to people, and having an impact on the world.

My passion for technology started at the age of 12 when I was tinkering on the Internet and built a number of websites that attracted hundreds of thousands of users. I then attended Babson College in the United States to major in Entrepreneurship & Finance. After graduation, I moved to Singapore and became a Software Engineer writing code for web and mobile products.

I spent 4 years at First Code Academy, a technology education company providing coding programs for kids aged 4 to 18 years old across Hong Kong, Singapore, Taipei, Shanghai, and Shenzhen. When I joined First Code, we had 5 people in Hong Kong and by the time I left my role as a COO, we had 35 full-time and about 50 part-time globally.

Recently, I was the co-founder and CEO of Toasty, a video meeting platform that focuses on collaboration and engagement. We aspired to turn online meetings and gatherings from a dull, one-way interaction to a collaborative experience.

I am a generalist in business, that includes developing new markets, driving product growth (my current focus), marketing and customer success, recruiting and training, automating processes etc.

My life motto: Comfort is the Enemy of Growth.

I enjoy: Humble & fair, develop people, drive sustainable growth, and nurture a learning-oriented culture

Not my cup of tea: Too much ego, self-centeredness, lack of transparency, manual processes, short-sighted

I speak regularly at schools, events, and conferences about business, education, technology and coding which you can find out more here. When I'm not working on something, I am likely to be reading on my Kindle, running or cycling outdoors, organizing Babson alumni events or entrepreneurial meetups, or embracing my role as a father and husband.
